Advanced Topics in Foundation Models

Course Description: This advanced graduate seminar explores the cutting edge of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Multimodal Foundation Models. While the course establishes a baseline with state-of-the-art autoregressive (AR) systems, the primary focus is on moving beyond standard architectures to examine next-generation paradigms. Key topics include hierarchical reasoning, latent space planning, and non-autoregressive generation. The curriculum emphasizes both empirical advances and theoretical understanding, drawing heavily on recent breakthroughs. The seminar will also feature guest lectures from researchers at leading industry labs and top-tier universities.

⚠️ Prerequisites & Research Expectations

This is a research-intensive course. Students are expected to produce novel research artifacts suitable for submission to top-tier workshops or conferences.

  • Deep Learning Foundations: Solid understanding of neural network architectures (CNNs, RNNs) and optimization. Crucial: You must be intimately familiar with the Transformer architecture (Self-Attention, Multi-head attention, Positional embeddings).
  • Programming Proficiency: High proficiency in Python and PyTorch is non-negotiable. You should be comfortable managing environments, debugging tensor mismatches, and using Hugging Face libraries.
  • Research Commitment: The Final Project has a high bar. Students must be willing to dedicate significant time outside of class to reading raw arXiv papers, writing code, and running large-scale experiments.
